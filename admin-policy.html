Data Security Policy

Last updated: November 5, 2025

Data Classification & Handling

ACTIVE

Policy Statement

All company data must be classified according to its sensitivity and handled appropriately to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability. This policy establishes the framework for data classification and defines security controls for each classification level.

Data Classification Levels

  • Confidential: Highly sensitive data that could cause severe damage if disclosed (e.g., source code, financial records, customer PII)
  • Internal Use: Business data not intended for public disclosure (e.g., internal communications, project documentation)
  • Public: Information approved for public release (e.g., marketing materials, public website content)

Security Requirements

  • Encryption at rest and in transit for Confidential data
  • Multi-factor authentication for accessing sensitive systems
  • Regular access reviews and privilege audits
  • Secure disposal methods for data at end-of-life
  • Monitoring and logging of access to sensitive data

Access Control Policy

ACTIVE

Policy Statement

Access to TechSphere Systems resources must be granted based on the principle of least privilege, ensuring users have only the access necessary to perform their job functions.

Access Management

  • User access must be reviewed quarterly
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) must be implemented
  • Administrative accounts require separate credentials
  • Access must be revoked within 24 hours of role change or termination
  • Privileged access must be logged and monitored

Incident Response Policy

DRAFT

Policy Statement

TechSphere Systems must have a formal incident response capability to quickly detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents, minimizing business impact.

Incident Classification

  • Severity 1: Critical impact - Full incident response team activation
  • Severity 2: High impact - Limited team activation with executive notification
  • Severity 3: Medium impact - Standard response procedures
  • Severity 4: Low impact - Documentation and minor remediation

System Administration Policy

ACTIVE

Policy Statement

System administrators must follow standardized procedures for managing and maintaining all company systems to ensure security, reliability, and availability.

Administration Guidelines

  • All administrative actions must be logged and auditable
  • System changes must follow the change management process
  • Regular security patches must be applied within 30 days of release
  • Backup procedures must be tested quarterly
  • Emergency access procedures must be documented and tested annually

User Account Policy

ACTIVE

Policy Statement

User account management must follow standardized procedures to ensure proper access control, security, and accountability across all systems.

Account Management

  • Unique user accounts must be created for each individual
  • Password complexity requirements must be enforced
  • Accounts must be disabled after 90 days of inactivity
  • Access reviews must be conducted semi-annually
  • Terminated employee accounts must be disabled immediately
